Section 1: Understanding Wildfires

What causes wildfires and how they spread

What is a Wildfire?

A wildfire is an unplanned, uncontrolled fire that burns in natural areas like forests, grasslands, or brushlands.

🔥 The Fire Triangle

Heat
Ignition source (lightning, human activity)
Fuel
Vegetation, trees, dry grass
Oxygen
Air - especially in windy conditions

🔥 Fire Danger Levels

Low Green

Fuels do not ignite easily. Controlled burns are safe.

Moderate Blue

Some fire risk. Be careful with outdoor burning.

High Yellow

Fuels dry. Fire can spread quickly. Restrictions likely.

Very High Orange

Extreme caution. Fire spreads rapidly. Burn bans active.

Extreme Red

DANGER! Fire explodes, spotting occurs. Prepare to evacuate.

Key Insight: 90% of wildfires are caused by humans. Always follow fire safety guidelines!

Advanced Topic: Fire Weather

Understanding Red Flag Warnings

Authorities issue a Red Flag Warning when conditions are "just right" for a wildfire to start and spread rapidly:

High Heat
Low Humidity
High Winds
Dry Lightning

Section 2: Wildfire Behavior

How wildfires spread and move

Uphill Spread

Fires spread faster uphill (preheating fuels above). Can double speed for every 10° slope!

Wind-Driven

Wind is the most important factor - fires can spot 1+ miles ahead of main fire.

Fire Whirls/Firenadoes

Rotating columns of fire that can lift debris and spread embers miles away.

Pyrocumulonimbus Clouds

Massive fire-generated thunderstorms that create lightning and more fires.

Watch Out: Fires can move up to 14 mph - faster than you can run uphill!

Section 3: Preparation - Defensible Space

Protect your home before fire season

Zone 0: Immediate (0-5 ft)
  • Remove all dead plants/weeds
  • Remove branches overhanging roof
  • Clean leaves from gutters
  • Move firewood away
Zone 1: Lean (5-30 ft)
  • Space trees 30+ feet apart
  • Remove ladder fuels (low branches)
  • Keep grass mowed under 4 inches
  • Use fire-resistant plants
Zone 2: Reduced (30-100 ft)
  • Remove dead/dying trees
  • Thin dense vegetation
  • Maintain driveway clearance
  • Create fuel breaks
🏠 Home Hardening Tips
Class A fire-rated roofing
1/8" mesh screens on vents
Tempered glass windows
Non-combustible siding

Section 4: Evacuation Planning

Know when to go and where to go

EVACUATION ORDER - LEAVE IMMEDIATELY!

Danger to your area is imminent or occurring. Do NOT wait!

EVACUATION WARNING - Get Ready!

Potential threat. Pack your go-bag and prepare to leave.

ALL CLEAR - Safe to Return

Fire is contained. Follow official instructions for re-entry.

📋 Evacuation Checklist
  • Know 2+ evacuation routes
  • Plan meeting place outside fire zone
  • Prepare Go-Bag (keep by door)
  • Download emergency apps
  • Sign up for local alerts
  • Know pet evacuation plan
  • Keep gas tank at least half full
  • Practice drill with family

Section 5: During the Wildfire

Critical survival actions

LEAVE EARLY - DON'T WAIT!

If told to evacuate, leave immediately. Roads can become impassable within minutes.

🚗 If You Can Evacuate Safely

1
Take your Go-Bag and emergency supplies
2
Wear protective clothing (long pants, long sleeves, boots, N95 mask)
3
Close windows and doors but leave unlocked for firefighters
4
Leave lights on so house is visible in smoke
5
Move to designated evacuation center or safe area

🏠 If Trapped at Home (Shelter-in-Place)

1
Stay inside away from exterior walls - close all doors
2
Fill bathtubs and sinks with water (to drink and wet towels)
3
Stay low to ground where air is cooler and cleaner
4
Call 911 and give your exact location
NEVER: Wait to evacuate hoping fire will change direction. Never drive through heavy smoke.

Section 6: Wildfire Go-Bag

Essential supplies for rapid evacuation

Water & Food
  • 1 gallon water/person (3 days)
  • Energy bars & non-perishable food
  • Water purification tablets
  • Manual can opener
Medical & Safety
  • First aid kit + medications
  • N95 masks (smoke protection)
  • Flashlight + batteries
  • Portable charger/power bank
Protective Gear
  • Sturdy boots & wool/cotton clothes
  • Work gloves & goggles
  • Emergency blanket
  • Change of clothes
Documents & Essentials
  • ID/Passport (waterproof bag)
  • Insurance & medical cards
  • Cash (small bills)
  • Phone & charger
  • List of emergency contacts
Pro Tip: Keep your Go-Bag in your car or by the front door. Add a whistle and bright cloth for signaling.

Section 7: After the Wildfire

Staying safe during recovery

Wait for official "All Clear" - Do not return until authorities say it's safe
Hidden Dangers
  • Hot ash pits (can remain hot for days)
  • Unstable trees (danger of falling)
  • Damaged gas/electric lines
  • Contaminated water supplies
  • Ash containing toxic chemicals
Safety Steps
  • Wear N95 mask and gloves
  • Wet ash before cleaning (avoid dust)
  • Check for gas leaks - leave if you smell gas
  • Document damage for insurance
  • Boil water until safe
Health Warning: Ash contains hazardous materials. Children, elderly, and those with breathing issues should avoid cleanup areas.
