Master the critical life-saving steps for tornado season.

1. Watch vs. Warning

Understanding the difference between a "Watch" and a "Warning" can save your life.

Tornado Watch

Be Prepared! Tornadoes are possible in and near the watch area. Review and discuss your emergency plans and check supplies and your safe room.

Tornado Warning

Take Action! A tornado has been sighted or indicated by weather radar. There is imminent danger to life and property. Move to an interior room on the lowest floor.

2. The Enhanced Fujita (EF) Scale

Tornadoes are rated based on the damage they cause, which correlates to wind speed.

3. Choosing a Shelter

The best place to be is underground or in a reinforced safe room.

Safe Locations
  • Basement or Storm Cellar
  • Interior room on lowest floor (no windows)
  • Sturdy building with a strong foundation
Dangerous Locations
  • Mobile homes (even if tied down)
  • Under a highway overpass (wind tunnel)
  • Rooms with windows or external walls

4. Emergency Drills: Drop, Cover, Hold On

When a warning is issued, you may have only seconds to react.

1
DROP to your knees. This keeps you from being knocked over.
2
COVER your head and neck with your arms. If a sturdy table is nearby, crawl under it.
3
HOLD ON to your shelter until the shaking or wind stops.

5. Identifying Signs

Sometimes tornadoes occur without a warning. Watch for these natural signs:

  • Dark, often greenish, sky
  • A loud roar, similar to a freight train
  • Large hail in the absence of rain
  • Debris cloud or rotating funnel

6. After the Tornado

Safety continues after the storm passes.

  • Stay away from downed power lines.
  • Check for gas leaks (smell of rotten eggs).
  • Wear sturdy shoes and gloves when clearing debris.
  • Stay out of damaged buildings until authorities say they are safe.
