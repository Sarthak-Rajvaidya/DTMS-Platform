🌊 Flood Safety & Response

Learn how to prepare for, respond to, and recover from flooding events.

Course Progress 0%

1. Types of Flooding

Not all floods are the same. Understanding the cause helps you prepare.

Flash Floods: Rapid flooding in low-lying areas, often caused by heavy rain or dam failure.
River Floods: Occur when river levels rise over their banks due to persistent rain or snowmelt.
Coastal Floods: Caused by storm surges, high tides, or tsunamis in coastal areas.

2. Understanding Alerts

Knowing the difference between a watch and a warning is critical.

Flood Watch

Be prepared! Flooding is possible in your area. Stay tuned to local news and weather reports.

Flood Warning

Take action! Flooding is occurring or imminent. Evacuate if directed or move to higher ground.

3. Preparing Your Home

Minimize damage before the water arrives.

  • Elevate critical utilities (furnace, water heater)
  • Install sump pumps with battery backups
  • Use sandbags to block low-level entry points
  • Move valuables to the highest floor

4. Turn Around, Don't Drown

Most flood-related deaths occur in vehicles. Never drive into water.

Did you know?

Just 6 inches of moving water can knock you off your feet. 12 inches of water can carry away a small car.

5. Emergency Go-Bag

Ensure you have these essentials ready for immediate evacuation.

Clean Water
Non-perishables
First Aid Kit
Flashlight
Power Bank
Documents

6. During the Flood

Stay safe when the water arrives.

  • 🚶 Move to higher ground immediately.
  • 🔌 Turn off electricity at the main breaker if safe.
  • 🚱 Avoid contact with floodwater (it may be contaminated).
  • 📻 Stay tuned to local emergency radio stations.

7. Recovery & Return

Returning home safely after the water recedes.

Only return when authorities declare the area safe. Be aware of hidden hazards:

⚠️ Structural damage to your home.
⚠️ Mold growth within 24-48 hours.
⚠️ Contaminated well water or tap water.
