🫨 Earthquake Safety & Survival

Learn the critical actions for when the ground starts to move.

Course Progress 0%

1. Understanding Earthquakes

Earthquakes are caused by the sudden release of energy in the Earth's crust that creates seismic waves.

Plate Tectonics

The Earth's surface is broken into large pieces called tectonic plates. When these plates slide past, collide, or pull apart, they create stress that eventually releases as an earthquake.

2. The First 60 Seconds

The most dangerous time during an earthquake is the initial shaking. Your immediate actions determine your safety.

Drop, Cover, and Hold On!

Do not try to run outside or stand in a doorway. Most injuries occur as people try to move during the shaking.

3. Finding Safe Spots

Identify safe places in each room before the shaking starts.

Safe Spots
  • Under heavy desks or tables
  • Against interior walls
  • Corners of a room
Danger Zones
  • Near windows or glass
  • Tall furniture (bookshelves)
  • Hanging objects (mirrors)

4. Magnitude vs. Intensity

Earthquakes are measured in two different ways:

Magnitude (Richter Scale)

Measures the energy released at the source. It is a single value for each earthquake.

Intensity (Mercalli Scale)

Measures the strength of shaking at a specific location. It varies depending on distance.

5. Hazard Mitigation

Prepare your home to reduce risk before an earthquake strikes.

  • Anchor tall furniture to wall studs.
  • Install latches on cabinet doors.
  • Store heavy items on lower shelves.
  • Know how to shut off gas and water.

6. Aftershocks & Safety

Earthquakes are often followed by smaller tremors called aftershocks.

Stay Alert!

Aftershocks can happen minutes, days, or even months after the main event. They can cause further damage to already weakened structures.
